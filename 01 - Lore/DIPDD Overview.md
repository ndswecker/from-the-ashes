# DIPDD – Overview
*Digital-Induced Perceptual Deterioration Disorder*  
*(Common Name: The Veil Syndrome)*

## Summary
Digital-Induced Perceptual Deterioration Disorder (DIPDD), commonly called *The Veil Syndrome*, is a modern psychiatric-neurological condition characterized by progressive sensory detachment, emotional blunting, and dissociation triggered by prolonged digital media exposure.

Doctors identify DIPDD as a disruptive dissociative disorder involving perception, identity, and bodily self-awareness.

## Symptoms
DIPDD progresses through distinct stages, each marked by increasing detachment:

- **Stage 1: Early Dissociative Drift**  
  Colors dull, sounds mute, emotions weaken; world feels “behind glass.”

- **Stage 2: Perceptual Decoupling**  
  Bodily self-recognition fades; patients describe feeling outside themselves.

- **Stage 3: Identity Dilution**  
  Shadowed periphery, emotional collapse, lapses in selfhood.

- **Stage 4: Gossamer State**  
  Patient becomes numb and motionless; body and soul misaligned.

- **Stage 5: Digital Depersonalization Collapse (DDC)**  
  A waking coma—patient can move and eat but lacks consciousness entirely.  
  From a spiritual perspective, their soul has fully slipped.
